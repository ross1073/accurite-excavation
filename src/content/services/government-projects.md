## Government Excavation Contractor in Utah

AccuRite Excavating has been a trusted government excavation contractor in Utah for over 31 years. We hold the credentials, bonding, and compliance documentation that federal, state, and municipal contracts require. From military installations to national park infrastructure, we've completed public works projects that demand more than just equipment. They demand accountability.

If you're a contracting officer or public works director reviewing bids, here's what you need to know about us.

## Credentials and Compliance

**E100 Excavation Contractor License:** Utah's highest-level excavation contractor designation, covering the full scope of earthwork, grading, and underground utility work required on public projects.

**Bonding:** We carry performance and payment bonds appropriate for government and public works contract thresholds. Bonding documentation is available with bid submissions.

**Insurance:** Full general liability, workers' compensation, and equipment coverage. Certificates of insurance are provided as required by contract specifications.

**Davis-Bacon Act compliance:** We maintain certified payroll records and comply with prevailing wage requirements on all federally funded projects where Davis-Bacon applies.

**Security clearance coordination:** Several of our key personnel have experience working on controlled military installations. We follow installation security protocols and can work within the access requirements of federal facilities.

## Featured Government Projects

### Military Installations

AccuRite has completed excavation and site work at Camp Williams and Hill Air Force Base. Military projects require strict adherence to installation security procedures, schedule coordination with base personnel, and documentation requirements that go beyond standard commercial contracts. We've navigated these requirements successfully on multiple projects.

### Army Corps of Engineers

Site work projects coordinated through the Army Corps of Engineers involve detailed specifications and inspection requirements. We've completed projects to Corps standards and understand the documentation and inspection process these contracts require.

### National Park Service

NPS projects often involve environmentally sensitive sites where excavation work must be done carefully to protect natural features, archaeological zones, or drainage patterns. We've worked on infrastructure projects at National Park Service sites and understand the additional protocols involved.

### U.S. Postal Service

Facility site development for USPS properties requires meeting federal construction standards and working within the operational constraints of active postal facilities. We've completed parking, grading, and site improvement projects for USPS locations in Utah.

### Ogden City Airport

The Ogden-Hinckley Airport is a public facility with airside and landside infrastructure demands. Site work at an airport requires coordination with FAA requirements, airport operations staff, and safety protocols that protect aircraft movement areas. We've completed work at this facility.

### Weber County Engineering

County public works projects, including road improvements, drainage infrastructure, and facility site work, are a regular part of our project portfolio. We work with Weber County Engineering on projects throughout unincorporated Weber County.

## What Government Site Work Involves

Public works excavation projects typically include one or more of the following scopes.

**Grading and site preparation:** Building pads, parking areas, access roads, and facility grounds for government facilities require precise grading to design elevations with proper drainage and compaction.

**Underground utility installation:** [Underground utilities](/services/underground-utilities) for government facilities follow the same basic process as commercial projects but with more detailed specifications, inspection hold points, and documentation requirements.

**Demolition and site clearing:** Many government projects involve removing existing infrastructure before new construction begins. Our [demolition services](/services/demolition) cover concrete removal, structure demolition, and site clearing.

**Drainage and erosion control:** Federal and state projects require storm water pollution prevention plans and erosion control measures that meet EPA and state DEQ standards.
