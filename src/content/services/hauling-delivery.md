## Materials We Deliver

### Gravel and Crushed Rock

Gravel is the most common material we deliver. The type matters for your application.

**Road base (3/4" minus compactable):** The standard material for driveways, parking pads, and construction access roads. It contains fines that pack down tight when compacted. Most residential driveway jobs use road base.

**3/4" clean crushed rock:** Clean rock without fines, used for drainage applications, French drains, leach fields, and areas where water needs to move through the rock layer. It doesn't compact well, which is the point.

**Pea gravel:** Small rounded stone used for walkways, playgrounds, drainage, and decorative applications. Common in Weber County landscaping.

**Rip rap and cobble:** Larger angular rock used for erosion control on slopes, stream banks, and drainage channels. Also used decoratively.

### Fill Dirt

Fill dirt is subsoil material used to raise grade elevations, fill holes, or build up areas for construction. It doesn't contain the organic material that topsoil does. Fill dirt compacts well, which makes it appropriate for subgrade applications under driveways, slabs, and foundations.

The quality of fill dirt matters. We provide clean fill, not debris-laden or contaminated material.

### Topsoil

Topsoil is what you need for lawns, gardens, and landscaping. It's the nutrient-rich upper soil layer that supports plant growth. If you're reseeding a yard, establishing a garden bed, or finishing a landscaping project, topsoil is what goes on top.

We deliver screened topsoil to Weber and Davis County addresses.

### Sand

Sand is used for leveling pavers and flagstone, playground fill, drainage applications, and mortar mix jobs. We deliver in dump truck quantities for larger projects.

### Boulders and Decorative Rock

We deliver landscape boulders, feature rocks, and decorative stone for yard and garden projects. Larger boulders may need equipment for placement, which we can coordinate.

## Calculating How Much Material You Need

The math isn't complicated, but it trips people up.

**For gravel and fill dirt:**
1. Measure the area in length x width (in feet)
2. Decide on depth (in inches), then divide by 12 to get feet
3. Multiply length x width x depth to get cubic feet
4. Divide by 27 to get cubic yards
5. Multiply cubic yards by 1.4 to get approximate tons

A 20x30 foot driveway at 4 inches deep works out to roughly 9 tons of road base. When in doubt, call us with your measurements and we'll calculate it.

**Minimum delivery:** Most deliveries are a minimum of one truck load, which is 10-14 tons depending on material density.
