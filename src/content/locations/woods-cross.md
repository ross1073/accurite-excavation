Woods Cross doesn't look like the rest of Davis County. Where most neighboring cities are predominantly residential with a commercial strip, Woods Cross has an industrial spine running through its center. The Holly Refinery, rail yards, and industrial properties along I-15 have shaped this city since before the freeway was built. East of the corridor, residential neighborhoods are established and aging. The excavation work here reflects that dual character — we do residential foundation work and utility replacement in the neighborhoods, and commercial demolition and site prep along the industrial corridor.

## The Industrial Corridor

The I-15 corridor through Woods Cross is one of the most industrially active stretches in Davis County. The Holly Refinery complex anchors the west side, with rail infrastructure, warehousing, and commercial properties filling in along both sides of the freeway.

Excavation work in this corridor is different from residential neighborhoods. Commercial [demolition](/services/demolition) projects require coordinated utility shutoffs, environmental clearances, and debris management at a different scale. Site prep for new commercial construction on previously developed land often involves removing old foundations, abandoned utility lines, and fill material of uncertain composition.

We handle commercial excavation and demolition in the Woods Cross industrial zone, including:

- Building demolition and removal
- Foundation and concrete slab removal
- Site clearing and grading for redevelopment
- Utility trenching and relocation
- Debris hauling to approved disposal sites

Some corridor properties have environmental histories that require specialized handling. We work alongside environmental consultants when contaminated soil management is part of the project. Our role is the physical excavation — the environmental assessment and monitoring are handled by qualified environmental professionals.

## Residential Neighborhoods East of I-15

The residential side of Woods Cross is a different world from the corridor. Neighborhoods along 500 South, Center Street, and the blocks climbing east toward Bountiful have homes dating from the 1950s through the 1990s, with some newer infill construction.

The soil here is standard Bonneville clay on the lower streets, transitioning to rocky alluvium as you move east and uphill toward the Bountiful border. Residential excavation work includes foundation digs for new construction and additions, utility replacement for aging infrastructure, and drainage improvements for homes affected by the clay's moisture reactivity.

### Aging Infrastructure

Woods Cross's older neighborhoods have utilities that are reaching the end of their service life. Original sewer laterals, water service lines, and storm drains installed in the 1960s and 1970s need replacement. We handle [underground utility](/services/underground-utilities) work throughout these neighborhoods, from individual service line replacements to larger main line projects.

Trenching in established neighborhoods means working around mature trees, existing driveways, and other utilities. We plan routes to minimize disruption and restore disturbed surfaces to their pre-work condition.

### Foundation Work on Older Homes

Homes built in the 1950s and 1960s in Woods Cross have foundations that may need reinforcement, waterproofing, or partial replacement. The Bonneville clay has been working against these foundations for decades — the seasonal swelling and shrinking creates stress that eventually cracks concrete. We do excavation for foundation repair and waterproofing, digging down to the footing to allow repair crews access to the exterior wall.

## The 500 South Corridor

The 500 South corridor through Woods Cross carries significant traffic and has seen commercial development and redevelopment. Site prep for commercial projects along this corridor follows standard Bonneville clay protocols — proper compaction, drainage, and subgrade preparation.

Commercial [grading and site prep](/services/grading-land-clearing) along 500 South is straightforward terrain-wise but requires attention to traffic management and access during construction. We plan our work to maintain traffic flow and access to adjacent businesses.

## Rail Corridor Considerations

The Union Pacific rail line runs through Woods Cross parallel to I-15. Properties adjacent to the rail corridor may have been affected by historical rail operations, and excavation near the tracks requires coordination with the railroad and compliance with right-of-way setbacks.

We've worked near the rail corridor and understand the notification and clearance requirements. For projects within the railroad's zone of influence, we coordinate ahead of time to avoid delays.

## Stormwater and Drainage

Like most valley floor communities, Woods Cross has drainage challenges related to the flat terrain and clay soil. The city has been investing in stormwater infrastructure, and excavation projects contribute to this effort through proper site grading and connection to the stormwater system.

We grade every project site — residential and commercial — to direct water away from structures and toward the stormwater system. For properties with chronic drainage issues, we install French drains, regrading, and catch basins to redirect water.
